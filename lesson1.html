☁️ Core Concept

What is Cloud Computing?

Instead of saving files on your computer's hard drive, cloud computing lets you store, access, and share data through the internet — from any device, anywhere.

💡 Think of it like this: instead of keeping your accounting files in a cabinet at your desk, you keep them in a secure online vault that your whole team can access from anywhere.

🔑 Key Concept

Traditional Software vs. SaaS

💿

Traditional Software

Installed on your computer. Manual updates. Data stored locally. Examples: Excel desktop, old accounting software.

☁️

SaaS

Accessed via browser. Always up to date. Data stored in the cloud. Examples: QuickBooks Online, Xero, Google Workspace.

⚠️

Why It Matters

SaaS means your data lives on someone else's servers — making access control and sharing settings critically important.

🎯 Why Accountants Are Targeted

Accounting Data is a High-Value Target

🪪

PII

Names, addresses, Tax Identification Numbers (TIN)

🏦

Financial Records

Bank account numbers, credit card data, transaction history

📊

Business Intelligence

Revenue figures, cost structures, contracts, pricing

💼

Payroll Data

Salaries, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG records

⚠️ A single misconfigured Google Drive folder containing payroll data can expose hundreds of employees' personal and financial information — leading to identity theft, fraud, and legal liability.

📋 Lesson 1 Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Cloud computing stores data on internet-accessible servers, not your local device
  • SaaS tools like Teams, Slack, QuickBooks Online are central to modern accounting
  • Accounting data — PII, financials, payroll — is among the most targeted by attackers
  • Cloud providers secure infrastructure; you secure access and sharing
  • One misconfiguration can expose hundreds of people's sensitive data
