Compliance &
Legal Context

Philippine laws that govern how accountants must protect cloud data

⚖️ Why It Matters

Compliance is Not Optional

As an accounting professional in the Philippines, you are legally obligated to protect the data you handle. Ignorance of the law is not a valid defense.

🏛️

Legal Liability

You — not just your employer — can be held personally liable for data breaches caused by negligence.

💰

Financial Penalties

Fines can reach ₱2,000,000 per violation under the Data Privacy Act.

⛓️

Criminal Charges

Serious violations can result in imprisonment of 1 to 6 years for responsible officers.

🏛️ Republic Act 10173

Data Privacy Act of 2012

  • Requires organizations to implement reasonable and appropriate security measures for personal data
  • Cloud misconfigurations that expose PII (names, TINs, salaries) are direct violations
  • Organizations must report data breaches to the NPC within 72 hours
  • Individuals have the right to know what data is collected and how it is used
  • Applies to all organizations processing personal data — including accounting firms

📋 BIR & SEC

Record Keeping & Financial Reporting

🏦
BIR Regulations
Accounting records must be retained for a minimum of 10 years. Cloud storage must ensure long-term data integrity. Accidental deletion of tax records can result in BIR penalties and surcharges.

✅ Enable version history and set retention policies in your cloud storage to automatically comply with BIR's 10-year retention requirement.

📊
SEC Memorandum Circulars
Financial reports submitted to the SEC must be accurate, tamper-proof, and verifiable. Cloud version control provides an audit trail of document changes. Unauthorized modifications — even accidental — can constitute falsification.

⚠️ If an unauthorized user edits a financial statement in a shared cloud folder, the integrity of that document is compromised — even if the change was minor.

🏛️ BSP & NPC

Banking Regulations & Privacy Commission

🏧
BSP Circulars (Banking & Finance)
Strict data security for financial institutions using cloud. Cloud vendor due diligence required. Data residency requirements — some financial data must be stored within the Philippines. Regular penetration testing required.
🛡️
National Privacy Commission (NPC)
Register data processing systems with the NPC. Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO). Report breaches within 72 hours. Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments before implementing new cloud tools.

📋 Lesson 6 Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Philippine law — not just best practice — requires you to protect cloud data
  • The Data Privacy Act imposes fines up to ₱5M and imprisonment up to 6 years
  • BIR requires 10-year retention — cloud storage must support this with version history
  • SEC requires tamper-proof financial records — cloud access controls are part of internal controls
  • BSP requires cloud vendor due diligence for financial institutions
  • Appoint a DPO, register with NPC, and report breaches within 72 hours
