☁️ Core Concept
What is Cloud Computing?
From your desk drawer to a secure online vault
🖥️ Traditional (Local)
💿 Files saved on your hard drive
🔌 Only accessible at your desk
🔄 Manual backups required
👤 One person at a time
❌ Lost if computer breaks
VS
☁️ Cloud (SaaS)
🌐 Files stored on secure servers
📱 Access from any device, anywhere
🔄 Auto-backup & version history
👥 Real-time team collaboration
✅ Always available, always updated

⚠️ Security Trade-off: Cloud = convenience + collaboration, but your data now lives on someone else's servers. Access control becomes your #1 responsibility.

HIGH RISK

🤝 Shared Responsibility
Who Secures What?
Cloud security is a two-way partnership
Cloud Provider
🏢 Physical servers & data centers
🌐 Network infrastructure
⚡ Platform uptime & availability
🔒 Encryption of stored data
🛡️ DDoS protection
YOU (Accountant)
🔑 Who has access to your files
⚙️ Sharing settings & permissions
🔐 Passwords and MFA
📤 What data you upload & share
🗑️ Removing old/unused access

✅ Google & Microsoft secure their servers. You are responsible for how you configure and share data within those platforms.

📋 Lesson 1 Summary
Key Takeaways
☁️Cloud stores data on internet servers — accessible from any device, anywhere
🛠️Teams, Slack, QuickBooks Online, Xero are central to modern accounting work
🎯Accounting data — PII, financials, payroll — is among the most targeted by attackers
🤝Cloud providers secure infrastructure; YOU secure access, sharing, and permissions
⚠️One misconfiguration can expose hundreds of people's sensitive data
