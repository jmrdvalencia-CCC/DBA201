Philippine
Legal Context

Data breach response isn't just best practice — it's the law. Here's what RA 10173, NPC Circular 16-03, and PICPA standards require of you as a CPA.

Part 7.1 · RA 10173
Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173)
The foundational Philippine law governing personal data protection and breach notification.
🏛️ Breach Notification to NPC
Must be submitted within 72 hours of discovery. The clock starts when the breach is discovered — not when it occurred. Delayed discovery does not excuse delayed notification.
👥 Notification to Individuals
Affected individuals must be notified "within a reasonable period" after NPC notification. The notification must include what happened, what data was exposed, and what they should do to protect themselves.
⚡ What Triggers Notification
Any breach involving sensitive personal information OR any breach likely to cause harm to affected individuals. For accountants, virtually any client data breach qualifies.
⚠️ Penalties
Imprisonment: 1–6 years and Fines: ₱500,000–₱5,000,000. Penalties apply to both the organization AND individuals responsible for the breach or failure to report.
Part 7.2 · NPC Circular 16-03
NPC Circular 16-03: Breach Notification
The National Privacy Commission requires these 6 elements in every breach notification.
1
Nature of the Breach
What happened and what type of data was involved — personal, sensitive, financial, health-related
2
Date of the Breach
When the breach occurred AND when it was discovered — both dates are required
3
Scope of the Breach
Number of affected individuals — even an estimate is required if exact count is unknown
4
Measures Taken
Containment, eradication, and recovery steps already implemented or planned
5
Contact Person
Data Protection Officer (DPO) or authorized representative with direct contact information
6
Recommendations to Affected Individuals
What affected individuals should do to protect themselves — change passwords, monitor accounts, etc.
Part 7.3 · Professional Standards
PICPA Standards & Your CPA License
Beyond the law, your professional standards as a CPA create additional obligations during a breach.
🔒
Confidentiality
You are personally obligated to protect client data — even during a breach. Unauthorized disclosure, even accidental, can trigger professional sanctions.
🎓
Professional Competence
You must know how to respond to incidents. Ignorance is not a defense. Completing this training is part of your professional obligation.
⚡
Due Care
Delayed reporting or failure to follow procedures constitutes professional negligence. "I didn't think it was serious" is not an acceptable excuse.
🤝
Integrity
Honest, complete reporting is required — even if the breach was caused by your own mistake. Covering up a breach is far worse than causing one.
⚖️ Bottom Line: As a CPA, you are personally and professionally liable for how you handle a data breach. Proper reporting protects your clients, your firm, AND your career.
