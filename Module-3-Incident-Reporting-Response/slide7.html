Part 6.1 · The Safety Pyramid
Heinrich's Triangle — Predictive Intelligence
Originally from industrial safety (1931), now applied to cybersecurity. The ratio is consistent: for every catastrophic event, hundreds of warnings preceded it.
1Major Breach
10Minor Incidents
30Near-Misses
300Unsafe Behaviors
1 Major Breach — Data exposed, NPC notification, fines, lawsuits, client trust destroyed
10 Minor Incidents — Contained quickly, no confirmed data loss, but resources consumed
30 Near-Misses — Could have been breaches. Each one reveals a fixable vulnerability
300 Unsafe Behaviors — Weak passwords, sharing shortcuts, skipping MFA. The foundation of all incidents

Part 6.4 · Culture
The 5 Pillars of No-Blame Reporting Culture
Near-miss intelligence only works if people feel safe reporting. One punishment for honest reporting destroys years of culture-building.
🛡️
No Punishment
Never punish employees for reporting near-misses — even if they caused it
🏆
Celebrate Reports
Recognize reporters publicly. "Maria's report prevented a breach" builds culture
⚡
Act on Every Report
If nothing changes after reporting, people stop reporting. Visible action = trust
📢
Share Lessons
Anonymized lessons shared org-wide. One person's near-miss protects everyone
📋
Make It Easy
Simple form, multiple channels — email, chat, phone, anonymous option
💡 "If you see something, say something — and nothing bad will happen to you for saying it."
